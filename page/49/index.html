How Decisive are Break Points?

October 17, 2015
As part of the work I have been doing on the Pythagorean model for wins in tennis, I have—in-between MLB playoffs and the Shanghai Masters, in any case—been thinking a lot about break points lately. We all know break points are important. If a player doesn’t edge out his or her opponent’s break points won in a set, the set (and ultimately the match) will have to be decided in a tiebreak or, when a tiebreak isn’t in place, a match could go on ad infinitum as some of us thought might happen when big servers Isner and Mahut butted heads in the 2010 Wimbledon first round.
